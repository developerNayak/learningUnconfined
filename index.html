About Mentor

At the outset I would like to avail this opportunity to introduce myself. I am Tushar Deshpande. I teach English to students who are in pursuit of International curriculums like IB, IGCSE. I also guide students who are undergoing indigenous curriculums like CBSE and ICSE with English language skills. Apart from that, another feature of my repertoire is helping adult learners to acquire a sound grip on their communication and interactive skills, which are essential in today's competitive world. Armed with my graduation degree and an innovative marketing mind, I plunged straight into the ever so demanding and grueling corporate world. Although, I was doing reasonably well and getting a six figure salary, never the less, I wasn't waking up to anything exciting! Henceforth, my advent in the domain of pedagogy was propelled by two reasons: A. Dissatisfaction in corporate life. B. Presence of a teacher/mentor underlying in the intrinsic core of my being. But to awaken the mentor inside me was a big challenge as it required the sacrifice of materialistic gains provided by the alluring, jet set corporate lifestyle. But, let me tell you, that in hindsight, it was the best decision of my life. Why? You may ask! Well, simply because the joy of cracking a big marketing deal is nothing compared to nurturing a raw talent which might have gotten curbed due to the absence of a dedicated mentor.( Which I believe happens many times in the complex world that we live in). Today, the purpose of my life is well defined and extremely fulfilling. The challenges faced by the students today in their academic journey are far too many, but I feel that I mentor them to make it a joyous and a unique learning experience rather than a race which is to be won at any cost.

FAQ

What does the language program for kids focus on?

The said program emphasises on over all English development which includes, key areas like reading, writing and speaking

Which is more important: learning grammar or learning vocabulary?

Both are an essential aspect of possessing a good grip on any language and both go hand in glove.

My child speaks perfect English, yet is struggling badly with reading and writing assignments. Why is this?

While speaking we have the convenience of using a plenty of words to express our thought however, while writing we require to be more precise. That is where many of us lose the clutch on the effectiveness of a sentence.

As a parent how can I help my child to enhance is English language skills?

By encouraging him/her to read books, asking them to write short passages, and last but not least , , conversing with them in English on the home front

As an adult learner how quickly can I become proficient in the language?

Proficiency in any language is not an overnight process, but it can certainly be achieved through proper guidance, consecrated effort and firm determination.

Mission

"Striving to make a difference in every single human in quest of improvement”

I can unabashedly state, that as a student my performance in the area of academics was not too satisfactory. I understand there could have been several reasons contributing to that. One such reason was that, I wasn't able to understand clearly, whatever was being taught to me, or shall we say, owing to the educational system, none of my teachers were able to understand my needs and cater to them or perhaps, it could have been likewise, and that I was not able to spell out my needs effectively. I have no malice towards anyone but the harm which was done at that juncture has been done, and it has left an indelible scar on my inner self.

My entry into the sphere of teaching was inspired by the determination, to leave no stone unturned in helping students, especially the younger generation who are grappling with challenges like, Lack of Expression, Incoherent thought process, Inability to grasp information effectively and attempting to bring out the best in them, using language as a tool.

I believe, timely encouragement can facilitate individuals to scale great heights in their lives, and there is no such pleasure of being instrumental to it, because "for the world you could be someone, but for someone you could be the world".
